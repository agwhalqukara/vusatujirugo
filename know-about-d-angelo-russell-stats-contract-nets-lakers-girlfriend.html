Know About DAngelo Russell; Stats, Contract, Nets, Lakers, Girlfriend

Publish date: 2024-09-04

D’Angelo Russell is possibly the basketball’s brightest young stars with a superb career line in front of him. At the age of 24, the point guard has played for two different teams with extraordinary career stats.

American Basketball player, D’Angelo Russell wears the number one jersey for his current NBA team, the Brooklyn Nets. The team traded him and Timofey Mozgov with another player, Brook Lopez and Kyle Kuzma. In 2019, D’Angelo made a name at the All-Star selection.

D’ Angelo Russell Bio & Family

The player was born as D’Angelo Danté Russell on 23 February 1996 in Louisville, Kentucky. He was raised by his parents, Keisha Rowe, and Antonio Russell. D’Angelo went to Central High school in his hometown and then moved to Montverde Academy in Florida, a year later.

The Nets player stands at a tall height of 6 feet 5 inches with a body weight around 90 kgs.

Career & Net Worth

The 24-year-old player has a net worth of $2.5 million from his professional Basketball career since 2015. In his early rookie career, he got to play in various college programs together with the University of North Carolina, Michigan State, and Louisville. Back in 2015 while playing for Ohio State, his playing stats included an average of 19.3 points, 5.7 rebounds, 5.0 assists, and 1.6 steals in 35 games.

In the same following year, the Los Angeles Laker picked him for a rookie contract where Joe Harris also plays. He was paid a salary of $5,103,120 in the 2015/2016 by the Lakers and later an increment of the paycheck of $5,332,800 in the 2016/2017 season. D’Angelo made 63 appearances with an average of 15.6 points, 3.5 rebounds, and 4.8 assists despite his left injury during the season.

With his impressive playing stats at Lakers, the Brooklyn Nets during the 2017/2018 offered him a contract worth $5,562,360. In his debut game for the Nets on 18 October 2017, he made 30 points with five assists in a 140–131 loss to the Indiana Pacers. However, his knee injury occurred and kept him outside the court until early 2018. Three months later, he made his first career triple-double on 23 March 2018 during Lakers and Toronto Raptors game. With his majestic playing records, the Nets in their 2018/2019 season offered him an undisclosed contract around $7.02 million.

His Recent Stint; Golden State Warriors, Minnesota Timberwolves

On 7 July 2019, Russell was sent to another NBA side the Golden State Warriors as part of a sign-and-trade package for Kevin Durant.

Later, on 6 February 2020, he was traded to the Minnesota Timberwolves along with Jacob Evans and Omari Spellman for Andrew Wiggins. On 10 February, he debuted for Minnesota in a 137–126 loss to the Toronto Raptors, where he recorded 22 points and five assists.
